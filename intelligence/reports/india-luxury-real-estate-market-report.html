India Luxury Real Estate Market Report

The definitive annual report on India's luxury residential market — ₹3 Crore+ segment across Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad, and emerging markets.

GHAR.tv Research Intelligence Team
March 22, 2026 20 min read Updated Q1 2026

Key Takeaways

  • India's luxury residential market (₹3 Cr+) reached ₹2.8 Lakh Cr in 2025, growing 38% year-over-year.
  • 72% of luxury purchases were made by HNI end-users rather than investors, signalling a structural demand shift.
  • Bangalore surpassed Delhi NCR to become the second-largest luxury market after Mumbai.
  • Branded residences segment grew 45% as buyers increasingly favoured global hospitality-linked developments.
  • Ultra-luxury segment (₹10 Cr+) expanded 52%, led by Mumbai's South and Central corridors.
01

Executive Summary

India's luxury residential real estate market delivered a landmark performance in 2025, with total transaction value in the ₹3 Crore-and-above segment reaching ₹2.8 Lakh Crore. This represents a 38% increase over 2024, making it the strongest growth year since the post-pandemic recovery began. The market's expansion has been fuelled by a combination of wealth creation in the technology and financial services sectors, a generational shift in housing preferences, and the maturation of India's luxury development ecosystem.

₹2.8 Lakh Cr Total luxury market transaction value in 2025 — a 38% jump from ₹2.03L Cr in 2024

The defining narrative of 2025 has been the dominance of end-user demand. Unlike previous cycles where investor activity drove price appreciation, 72% of luxury purchases this year were made by high-net-worth individuals buying for personal use. This shift signals a fundamentally healthier market underpinned by genuine housing demand rather than speculative capital. Mumbai retained its position as the largest luxury market, but Bangalore's rapid ascent past Delhi NCR represents a meaningful realignment of India's luxury geography.

02

Market Size & Growth

The luxury residential segment has undergone a dramatic expansion over the past five years, accelerating from ₹0.95 Lakh Crore in 2021 to ₹2.8 Lakh Crore in 2025. New launches in the ₹3 Cr+ category rose to 14,200 units in 2025, while absorption rates climbed to 82%, indicating that demand is comfortably keeping pace with supply. 03

City-Wise Analysis

Mumbai continues to dominate India's luxury landscape with a 34% market share, driven by legacy wealth corridors in South Mumbai and emerging ultra-luxury hubs in Worli and BKC. However, the most significant development of 2025 is Bangalore's ascent to the second position, overtaking Delhi NCR on the strength of its technology-driven wealth creation engine.

Pune and Chennai, while smaller in absolute terms, are developing distinct luxury micro-markets that are drawing national developer interest.

04

Buyer Profile

The 2025 luxury buyer cohort marks a generational shift in India's real estate market. The median buyer age has dropped to 39 years from 46 in 2020, reflecting the emergence of first-generation wealth creators in technology, fintech, and professional services. These buyers prioritise design quality, sustainability credentials, and lifestyle amenities over purely speculative returns.

72% HNI end-users — the highest proportion of owner-occupier purchases in a decade

NRI buyers accounted for 18% of transactions, with strong flows from the UAE, the United States, and Singapore. Branded residences — projects developed in partnership with global hospitality brands — grew 45% year-over-year, suggesting that buyers are increasingly willing to pay a premium for internationally benchmarked living experiences. The average ticket size across the segment rose to ₹6.4 Crore, up from ₹5.1 Crore in 2024.

06

2026 Projections

Looking ahead, the luxury segment is projected to maintain strong momentum through 2026, with market size expected to reach ₹3.5–3.8 Lakh Crore. Key growth drivers include continued wealth creation in the technology sector, the formalisation of fractional ownership in luxury assets, and the entry of several international developers into the Indian market.

The luxury threshold is defined as ₹3 Crore and above. All figures are for the calendar year January–December 2025 unless otherwise stated.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What defines luxury real estate in India?

This report defines luxury real estate as residential properties priced at ₹3 Crore and above. The ultra-luxury sub-segment covers properties at ₹10 Crore and above. These thresholds are based on price-point analysis across India's top six metropolitan markets, capturing the top 5–8% of residential transactions by value.

Is luxury real estate a good investment in 2025?

The luxury segment has delivered 14–22% annualised capital appreciation across top micro-markets over the past three years, outperforming both mid-segment residential and several financial asset classes. However, luxury real estate is best suited for investors with a 5–7 year horizon, adequate liquidity reserves, and the ability to absorb higher transaction costs. End-use buyers benefit from both capital appreciation and lifestyle value.

How large is the ultra-luxury market (₹10 Cr+)?

The ultra-luxury segment (₹10 Crore and above) accounted for approximately ₹68,000 Crore in 2025, representing about 24% of the total luxury market by value. This segment grew 52% year-over-year, led by Mumbai's South and Central corridors, Bangalore's Whitefield–Sarjapur belt, and Gurugram's Golf Course Extension Road.
