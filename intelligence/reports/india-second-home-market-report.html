Executive Summary

India’s second-home market has emerged as one of the fastest-growing segments in the country’s real estate landscape. Fuelled by remote work normalisation, rising disposable incomes, and a post-pandemic lifestyle shift, the market crossed an estimated ₹42,000 crore in transaction value in FY 2025–26 - growing 28% year-on-year.

This report analyses 12 key destinations across hill stations, beach corridors, and heritage towns. We examine buyer demographics, pricing benchmarks, rental yield models, tax implications, and the infrastructure catalysts reshaping demand.

₹42,000 Cr Estimated market size of India’s second-home segment in FY 2025–26 - a 28% jump from the previous year

The second-home market is transitioning from a luxury indulgence to a serious investment asset class. Professional management platforms, fractional ownership models, and improved road/air connectivity have lowered the barrier to entry while boosting yields.

Market Size & Growth

The Indian second-home market was valued at approximately ₹18,000 crore in FY 2020, contracted to ₹12,000 crore during the pandemic in FY 2021, and has since rebounded dramatically. Post-pandemic demand was initially driven by “escape from the city” motivations, but has since matured into a structured investment thesis.

Growth Drivers

  • Remote work permanence: 42% of India’s tech workforce now operates in hybrid mode, enabling extended stays at second homes
  • Domestic tourism surge: Domestic tourist arrivals grew 18% in 2025, boosting occupancy rates for holiday homes
  • Infrastructure connectivity: New expressways (Mumbai–Goa, Delhi–Dehradun, Bangalore–Mysore) have cut travel times by 40–60%
  • Professional management: Platforms like SaffronStays, Vista Rooms, and StayVista now manage 15,000+ properties, making passive income viable
  • Fractional ownership: Platforms like hBits and PropertyShare have opened the ₹25–50 lakh entry point for shared luxury vacation homes

Legal & Tax Considerations

Buying a second home in India involves specific legal and tax considerations that differ from primary residences. Understanding these is critical for making an informed investment.

Income Tax Implications

  1. Deemed rental income: If your second home is not rented out, the income tax department may assess “deemed rental income” based on fair market rent. From FY 2025–26, this applies only if annual value exceeds ₹2.5 lakh.
  2. Home loan benefits: Interest on home loan for a second property is deductible under Section 24(b). For self-occupied property, the cap is ₹2 lakh/year. For rented property, there is no cap on interest deduction.
  3. Rental income taxation: Rental income is taxed under “Income from House Property” after a standard 30% deduction for repairs and maintenance.
  4. Capital gains: Long-term capital gains (held 2+ years) are taxed at 12.5% without indexation benefit (post Union Budget 2024). Short-term gains are taxed at slab rates.
  5. GST on purchase: For under-construction properties above ₹45 lakh, GST of 5% applies (without ITC). For ready-to-move-in properties, no GST.
ℹ️
State-Specific Regulations

Several states restrict property purchases by non-residents: Himachal Pradesh requires prior permission under Section 118 of the HP Tenancy & Land Reforms Act. Goa has no such restrictions for Indian citizens. Uttarakhand restricts agricultural land purchases. Always verify state-specific rules before investing.

Key Legal Checklist

  • Verify clear title - obtain a title search report from a local lawyer going back at least 30 years
  • Check land use/zoning - ensure the plot is zoned for residential/commercial use, not agricultural
  • RERA registration - mandatory for all under-construction projects; verify on the respective state’s RERA portal
  • Encumbrance certificate - confirms the property has no outstanding loans, mortgages, or liens
  • Municipal approvals - building plan sanction, occupancy certificate, completion certificate
  • Society/association rules - for gated communities, verify rental and Airbnb permissions in the bye-laws

NRI Buyers: Special Considerations

NRI buyers constitute 24% of the second-home market and bring unique advantages and challenges. The emotional connection to India, combined with currency advantage (especially for US/UK-based buyers), makes second homes an attractive investment class.

FEMA Regulations for NRI Property Purchase

  • NRIs and PIOs can purchase residential and commercial property in India without RBI approval
  • Agricultural land, farmhouse, and plantation property cannot be purchased without RBI permission
  • Repatriation of sale proceeds is permitted for up to 2 residential properties
  • Payment must be through NRO/NRE account or via normal banking channels from abroad
  • Power of Attorney (PoA) is essential for documentation when the buyer is not physically present

NRI demand for second homes in Goa has grown 45% year-on-year. They are buying not just for holidays but for eventual retirement and as a legacy asset for children. Managed villa operations make it a genuine passive income instrument.

- Ankit Jain, NRI Real Estate Advisory, GHAR.tv
⚠
Important for NRI Buyers

NRIs cannot purchase agricultural land or plantation property in India under FEMA regulations without explicit RBI permission. Always verify land classification before proceeding with any purchase.

Emerging Trends for 2026–28

  1. Branded residences enter the second-home space: Marriott, Accor, and ITC have announced branded villa projects in Goa, Alibaug, and Kasauli with 20–35% price premiums and guaranteed rental programs.
  2. Fractional ownership goes mainstream: Platforms like hBits, PropertyShare, and MYRE Capital now offer SEBI-registered fractional second-home products starting at ₹25 lakh with projected 12–15% IRR.
  3. Sustainable and off-grid homes: Buyer demand for solar-powered, rainwater-harvesting homes in Coorg, Kodaikanal, and Uttarakhand has grown 3x. IGBC-certified vacation homes command 15% premium.
  4. Workation infrastructure: Developers are building co-working spaces, high-speed fibre, and video-call-ready studios into second-home communities - blurring the line between vacation and remote office.
  5. Managed villa operators consolidate: SaffronStays, Vista Rooms, and StayVista are expanding from aggregation to development, launching their own branded villa communities with integrated management.
  6. Heritage conversions: Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, and Kerala are seeing a wave of heritage haveli and bungalow conversions into boutique second homes, combining cultural preservation with premium yields.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a second home a good investment in India in 2026?

Yes, particularly in destinations like Goa and Alibaug where rental yields of 5–8% combined with 12–18% capital appreciation make second homes a strong investment vehicle. Professional management platforms have made passive income from second homes genuinely viable.

Which is the best location for a second home in India?

The best location depends on your primary city and purpose. For Mumbai-based buyers, Alibaug and Lonavala offer weekend proximity. For Bangalore buyers, Coonoor and Coorg are ideal. For Delhi-NCR, Kasauli and Shimla work best. For pure investment, North Goa offers the highest combined yield and appreciation.

What are the tax implications of owning a second home?

From FY 2025–26, deemed rental income applies only if annual value exceeds ₹2.5 lakh. Home loan interest deduction is capped at ₹2 lakh under Section 24(b) for self-occupied properties, with no cap if rented out. Rental income gets a 30% standard deduction. Long-term capital gains are taxed at 12.5%.

Can NRIs buy a second home in India?

Yes. NRIs can purchase residential property in India under FEMA regulations without RBI approval. Repatriation of sale proceeds is permitted for up to 2 properties. Payment must be through NRO/NRE accounts. Agricultural land requires special RBI permission.

What is fractional ownership and is it safe?

Fractional ownership allows you to co-own a luxury property with other investors, typically through an SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle). SEBI-registered platforms like hBits and PropertyShare offer regulated products. Entry points start at ₹25 lakh with projected 12–15% IRR. Ensure the platform is SEBI-registered and the SPV structure is legally sound.

How do managed rental platforms work for second homes?

Platforms like SaffronStays, Vista Rooms, and StayVista handle everything: listing, marketing, guest management, check-in/out, housekeeping, and maintenance. They typically charge 18–25% of gross rental income. Owners retain the right to use the property (usually with 14–30 days blocked annually). Net yields after management fees are typically 60–70% of gross.

Should I buy a plot or a ready villa as a second home?

Ready villas start generating rental income immediately and carry lower execution risk. Plots offer higher long-term appreciation (20–30% in emerging corridors) but require construction investment and time. For investors seeking immediate yield, ready villas are preferred. For those with a 5+ year horizon and willingness to manage construction, plots in emerging corridors like South Goa and Coorg offer better value.
