MARKET OUTLOOK

India Real Estate Market Outlook 2025

A comprehensive data-driven overview of India's residential real estate — pricing trends, inventory, demand drivers, and segment-wise projections across top metros and Tier-2 markets.

GHAR.tv Research Intelligence Team
March 18, 2026 16 min read Updated Q1 2026

Key Takeaways

  • India's residential real estate market crossed ₹14.5 lakh crore in value, registering 12% year-on-year growth driven by sustained end-user demand.
  • The luxury segment (₹3 Cr+) grew fastest at 38%, reflecting rising affluence and NRI investment inflows across gateway cities.
  • Unsold inventory across top-8 cities declined by 18%, signalling healthier absorption and disciplined new supply.
  • Bangalore and Hyderabad led price appreciation, powered by IT-sector expansion and infrastructure upgrades.
  • The affordable housing segment (sub-₹50 lakh) saw muted 4% growth as input costs and land prices compressed margins.
01

Market Overview

India's residential real estate market demonstrated remarkable resilience in 2025, crossing the ₹14.5 lakh crore mark in total transaction value. This growth was underpinned by robust end-user demand, stable interest rates, and continued urbanisation across metro and Tier-2 cities. The year saw a structural shift away from speculative buying toward genuine homeownership, with first-time buyers accounting for nearly 55% of all transactions.

₹14.5 Lakh Cr Total residential real estate transaction value in 2025, marking a 12% increase over the previous year

Government policy initiatives including credit-linked subsidy extensions and stamp duty rationalisation in select states further supported demand. Infrastructure investments — particularly metro rail expansion and expressway connectivity — continued to unlock new micro-markets, redistributing growth beyond traditional central business districts.

02

City Performance

Performance varied significantly across India's top metro markets. Bangalore and Hyderabad emerged as frontrunners in price appreciation, driven by technology-sector employment growth and major infrastructure projects. Mumbai continued to lead in absolute transaction value, while Pune and Chennai posted steady gains. NCR saw a notable recovery in Gurugram and Noida corridors with improved buyer sentiment.

03

Segment Analysis

Segment-wise analysis reveals a clear bifurcation in demand patterns. The luxury and premium segments outperformed, while affordable housing growth moderated due to rising input costs. The mid-segment remained the volume driver, accounting for the largest share of overall transactions.

The mid-segment (₹50 lakh to ₹1 crore) continues to be the backbone of the market, driven by young professionals and nuclear families upgrading from rental or smaller homes. Developer focus has increasingly shifted to this sweet spot where demand visibility and pricing power converge.

04

Supply & Inventory

New launch activity remained buoyant with 3,42,000 units introduced across top-8 cities in 2025. However, developers exercised greater discipline compared to previous cycles, calibrating supply to absorption trends. This resulted in a significant 18% reduction in unsold inventory, bringing months-of-inventory to historically healthy levels.

3,42,000 New units launched across top-8 cities in 2025, with disciplined supply keeping inventory lean

RERA compliance and consolidation of the developer landscape have played a key role in improving supply quality. Branded developers with strong balance sheets captured a larger market share, while smaller players either exited or partnered through joint developments and land monetisation models.

05

Price Trends

Weighted average prices across the top-8 cities rose by 12% year-on-year, driven by a combination of genuine demand, reduced inventory overhang, and rising construction costs. Peripheral markets and new micro-markets along infrastructure corridors saw the sharpest appreciation, with select corridors in Bangalore and Hyderabad recording 20%+ gains.

The pricing upcycle is expected to sustain through 2026, albeit at a moderated pace of 8–10% as the base effect normalises. Markets with supply-demand equilibrium and strong employment fundamentals are positioned for the most resilient appreciation trajectory.

06

Outlook

The outlook for India's residential real estate market remains constructive heading into 2026. Key tailwinds include continued urbanisation, a young demographic profile, rising household incomes, and institutional capital inflows. Risks to monitor include potential interest rate volatility, regulatory changes, and global economic headwinds that could impact NRI demand and capital flows.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best city for real estate investment in 2025?

Bangalore and Hyderabad emerged as top performers for capital appreciation in 2025, driven by strong IT-sector employment, infrastructure expansion, and favourable supply-demand dynamics. However, the best city depends on your investment horizon, budget, and risk appetite. Mumbai offers stability and liquidity, while Pune and Chennai provide attractive entry points with solid growth potential.

Is the Indian real estate market in a bubble?

Current market indicators do not suggest a bubble. Unlike pre-2014 cycles, today's growth is driven by end-user demand rather than speculation. Unsold inventory has declined 18%, developer leverage is lower, RERA has improved transparency, and mortgage-to-GDP ratios remain well below global averages. Price growth, while strong, is supported by income growth and urbanisation fundamentals.

Which segment is best for first-time real estate investors?

The mid-segment (₹50 lakh to ₹1 crore) offers the best risk-reward balance for first-time investors. It has the highest absorption rates, strong rental demand, and broad buyer appeal for resale. Focus on locations along upcoming metro corridors or near established IT parks for the best combination of capital appreciation and rental yield.
